What is 3D Printing?

Three-dimensional (or 3D) printing is the process of using a plastic or plastic-infused materials, like PLA, ABS, Resin, etc., in order to build an object in three-dimensional space. There are multiple types of 3D printers, the two most common being a hot extrusion printer, where the plastic is pushed out through a hot nozzle, varying between 180 degrees Celsius and 250 degrees Celsius. The other most common type of printer is usually called a Resin Printer, and it is the process of using a liquid resin, which is highly sensitive to light, and usually a UV light in order to build the object that is wanted. These printers are more complicated to understand and use, however it is still a very popular 3D printer.

Habitat for Humanity: 3D Printed House

On Tuesday, December 21, at 10:30 a.m., Habitat for Humanity is going to be celebrating the first 3D printed house. This is the first 3D printed house that is fully livable on the east coast. This house is located in Williamsburg, Virginia.
This house is built in partnership with Alquist, a 3D printing home construction company. This company used the COBOD 3D Printer, which is a state of the art large scale printer. This home is approzimately 1,200 square-feet, with three bedrooms and two full baths. The build material of the house is solid concrete so there isn't going to be any worry about the house breaking down or blowing away. The company hopes to be able to continue their work all around the U.S., building homes for the future. Check out the full article on the WYDAILY website here.
